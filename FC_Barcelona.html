With the summer transfer window fast approaching, Barcelona must get to work quickly, in regards to their recruitment for the new season. After a somewhat promising start to Xavi’s spell at Camp Nou, the board is likely to heed the coach’s preferences and bring him players that cater to his system. To that end, Xavi has handed the Barcelona board a list of eight transfer targets ahead of the new season, as per Spanish journalist Xavi Torres. The list is led by the two free agents who have agreed personal terms with Barcelona.

In addition to the Brazilian, Bernardo Silva and Robert Lewandowski are the other stars added to the list. The Barcelona coach believes they have the quality to add a real edge to the team and help them compete in the UEFA Champions League next season. As for the defensive unit, it is no secret Xavi wants to sign a new central defender, in addition to Christensen, amid worries over Gerard Pique’s longevity. Jules Kounde remains the Spaniard’s top target in the summer, although Barcelona are also exploring the deal to sign Kalidou Koulibaly, a cost-effective alternative for the dynamic Frenchman.

With Barcelona expected to offload several players over the course of the summer transfer window, one of the stars who is likely to be on his way out is Riqui Puig.

The La Masia graduate generated a lot of excitement and expectations when he rose through the academy ranks and made his senior team breakthrough. However, things have not gone according to plans for the 22-year-old, as he has struggled to nail down a place in the side. Puig was frozen out of the team by Ronald Koeman, while things have failed to improve for him under Xavi Hernandez either. And, the Spaniard has been told by the manager that he is not going to be a part of his plans for the future.

As reported by Adrian Sanchez on Twitter, Barcelona winger Ousmane Dembele is inching closer to signing for Paris Saint-Germain in the summer, with the move ‘95% done’. The 25-year-old’s future has been a topic of much debate since last summer when he entered the final year of his contract. Hurdled by financial hardships, Barcelona have had to endure a few rusty weeks. It appears their current financial crisis has crippled their pursuit of several top targets including Raphinha.

A recent report from The Athletic’s Phil Hay has now offered another damning report on Barcelona’s waning prospects of signing the forward.

The highly-reliable Leeds United correspondent claims the Whites are sympathetic to Raphinha’s desire to leave Elland Road in the summer.

But with the forward still having two years remaining on his current contract, Leeds will not sell him on a cut-price deal. With the club also retaining its top-flight status, they are expected to demand around €70-80 million for the attacker.

After months of uncertainty, it appears Barcelona are set to go through with their plans to renew Sergi Roberto.

According to Mundo Deportivo, the midfielder could very well become a one-club man by penning a contract extension by next week. His terms will likely be low, as the board does not rate him highly due to his injuries and lack of game-time in the past.

Roberto was even on the verge of leaving Camp Nou at the end of the season. But it appears Xavi has played a key role behind Barcelona’s U-turn, with the head coach rating the midfielder highly due to his versatility.

The report suggests Xavi has identified three possible roles for Sergi Roberto ahead of the new season.

The Barcelona coach primarily wants Roberto to serve as a deputy for Sergio Busquets. He will be expected to step up, should the veteran require a rest throughout the course of next season.

Gerard Pique has been at the centre of controversy all throughout 2022, right from his Spanish Super Cup scandal to the problems in his personal life. RAC1 has only added to the pile, reporting a rather interesting velitation.

The reliable outlet says that Barcelona are hoping that Pique will announce his retirement at the end of the 2022/23 season so that they can avoid paying him €80 million in salary over the next two seasons.

The veteran defender, one of the highest-paid players in the world, was kind enough to defer his salary multiple times across the last three years to help Barcelona cope with the adverse financial impact of COVID.

He was also one of the first players in the team to take a pay-cut, which eventually helped the club register new signings in the summer of 2022.

While that has caused quite a row with Lionel Messi, it went down well with the Barcelona administration, as other team leaders followed suit.
